# Product

## Register

product

## Users

VoxM is for professionals, founders, consultants, developers, students, and small teams who join meetings in a browser tab and want a private, low-friction way to record, transcribe, and review what happened. They are usually between meetings or returning to a prior conversation, so the interface should help them find the right recording quickly, inspect evidence, and extract next steps without managing a heavy workspace.

## Product Purpose

VoxM records browser-tab meetings, captures local microphone audio, transcribes the audio with a user-provided provider key, and turns the meeting into local artifacts the user can inspect, export, retry, and trust. Success means a user can go from "I just finished a meeting" to "I know what was discussed, what was decided, what is pending, and where the raw recording is" without sending their entire meeting workflow to a hosted bot.

## Brand Personality

Private, precise, and work-focused. VoxM should feel like a quiet meeting evidence desk: controlled, legible, technical enough to be trusted, but not cold or theatrical.

## Anti-references

Do not make VoxM look like a generic marketing SaaS dashboard with oversized hero cards, decorative gradients, soft purple/blue blobs, or repeated metric tiles. Do not imitate bot-first meeting platforms that make the product feel like a social workspace before it feels like a reliable local tool. Avoid playful empty states, vague AI magic copy, and UI that hides failure details needed for debugging.

## Design Principles

- Evidence first: recordings, transcript, summary, errors, and export status should be inspectable without hunting.
- Local control: the interface should constantly reinforce that data is local-first and user-controlled.
- Fast review loop: the primary workflow is selecting a meeting and reviewing video, summary, transcript, and actions in one place.
- Plain operational language: labels should say exactly what the control does, especially for retries, exports, errors, and provider limits.
- Durable under failure: partial transcripts, failed chunks, missing video chunks, and invalid API keys should remain understandable and recoverable where possible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Preserve keyboard access for all core actions, visible focus states, reduced-motion behavior, readable text wrapping, and clear non-color status labels. Avoid hover-only functionality because users may be operating from trackpads, touch devices, or keyboard-only workflows.
